How the Pet Hostel Helped My Dog Overcome Separation Anxiety

Rajiv Shah Ahmedabad 15 Comments

Rajiv shares his heartfelt journey of helping his rescue dog, Coco, adapt to being away from home. Coco suffered from separation anxiety and would become restless whenever Rajiv left, even for a few hours. After trying several options, Rajiv discovered the pet hostel — a safe, friendly space that ultimately transformed Coco’s behavior.

“The staff was incredibly patient and trained,” says Rajiv. “They gave Coco time to socialize slowly, provided daily play sessions, and even sent me regular updates.” Over time, Coco began to enjoy her stay and returned home each time more relaxed and happy.

A Calming Environment that Feels Like Home

Coco's first few visits were short — just a few hours. The staff took special care to make her feel comfortable, letting her explore at her own pace. A designated calm zone helped reduce her anxiety, and slowly she began to trust her surroundings. By the third visit, she was running through the doors, tail wagging with excitement!

The personalized care and attention Coco received helped rebuild her confidence. From supervised playtime to soothing music during nap hours, every detail made a difference.

Why I Recommend This Pet Hostel

Leaving a pet behind is never easy. But knowing Coco is in good hands helps me travel with peace of mind. The facilities are clean, the team is professional, and the environment is warm and inviting. For pet parents dealing with anxiety or behavioral challenges, this place could be a game-changer.

I now confidently recommend this pet hostel to fellow dog parents. Whether it’s a weekend getaway or a long trip, Coco and I both feel at ease.

Rajiv Shah

I'm passionate about giving my rescue dog, Coco, the best care possible. Sharing my journey here to help other pet parents going through the same struggles.

Hi! I am Max

Max was a fluffy golden retriever who lived in a quiet suburb. Every morning, without fail, he would sit by the front window waiting for the same thing—the mailman. Now, most dogs bark at the mailman. Not Max. From the first day the mailman came, Max was enchanted by the man in the blue uniform. Perhaps it was the squeaky wheels of the cart, or the kind eyes behind his glasses. But every day, Max would wag his tail and press his nose against the glass, waiting. The mailman, whose name was Raj, noticed Max too. One day, he brought a treat. Max accepted it like royalty and gave a grateful bark. From then on, it became a tradition. Rain or shine, Raj would stop at Max’s gate, drop the mail, and hand Max a biscuit through the bars. One cold winter morning, Raj didn’t show up. Max waited. Morning turned into afternoon. Max didn’t eat, barely moved. The next day, the same. And the next. On the fourth day, a different mailman came. Max didn’t budge. But then, a week later, a familiar squeaky cart came up the street. It was Raj—on crutches. He had slipped on ice and broken his leg. But he said, “I had to come see my best buddy. Couldn’t stay away.” Max barked joyfully, tail wagging like mad. Raj handed him two biscuits this time—one for the wait. From that day on, their bond became something everyone on the street admired. Not just a dog and a mailman—but two friends who looked forward to seeing each other every single day.
